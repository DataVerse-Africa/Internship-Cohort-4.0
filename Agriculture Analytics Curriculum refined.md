## üéØ **Learning Objectives**

By the end of this track, interns will be able to:
- Analyze crop production and yield data
- Track farm costs and profitability
- Analyze agricultural market prices
- Monitor food availability and access
- Build farmer-friendly dashboards
- Understand seasonal patterns in agriculture
- Create simple farm budgets
- Communicate agricultural insights to farmers and organizations

## üìÖ **12-Week Curriculum Structure**

---

### **MONTH 1: FARM PRODUCTION & ECONOMICS**

---

#### **Week 1: Understanding Agricultural Data**

**What Interns Will Learn:**
- How farming works (planting ‚Üí growing ‚Üí harvesting ‚Üí selling)
- Basic agriculture terminology (yield, hectare, season, harvest)
- Types of crops and farming systems
- Where agricultural data comes from

**Self-Paced Research Topics:**
- What is crop yield?
- How do farmers make money?
- What is a farming season?

**Hands-On Activities:**
- Dataset: Simple farm records (crops, land size, harvest amounts)
- Exercise: Calculate yield per hectare
- Exercise: Identify which crops were planted
- Exercise: Track planting and harvest dates

**Deliverable:** 
- **Mini Project 1:** "Farm Production Overview"
  - Analyze 1 year of farm data
  - Calculate total production for each crop
  - Identify main crops grown
  - 2-page simple report

**Tools:** Excel, Google Sheets

---

#### **Week 2: Crop Yield Analysis**

**What Interns Will Learn:**
- How to measure farm productivity (yield per hectare)
- Compare yields across different farms
- Identify high-performing and low-performing farms
- Understand factors affecting crop performance

**Self-Paced Research Topics:**
- What makes some farms produce more?
- How to calculate yield properly?
- What is a good yield for common crops?

**SQL Focus:**
- SELECT and WHERE to filter crop data
- SUM and AVG for totals and averages
- GROUP BY for yields by farm or region
- Simple calculations in SQL

**Hands-On Activities:**
- Dataset: 500 farm production records
- Exercise: Calculate average yield per crop
- Exercise: Find top 10 highest-yielding farms
- Exercise: Compare yields across regions

**Deliverable:**
- **Project 1A:** "Crop Yield Dashboard"
  - Power BI dashboard showing:
    - Average yield by crop type
    - Yield by region
    - Top-performing farms
    - Seasonal production patterns
  - Simple insights (1 page)

**Tools:** SQL, Power BI, Excel

---

#### **Week 3: Farm Costs & Profitability**

**What Interns Will Learn:**
- Farm expenses (seeds, fertilizer, labor, land prep)
- Farm income (crop sales)
- How to calculate profit
- Which crops are most profitable

**Self-Paced Research Topics:**
- What are the main costs in farming?
- How do farmers price their crops?
- What is farm profitability?

**Excel Focus:**
- Simple farm budget template
- Revenue minus costs = profit
- Profit per hectare calculations
- Break-even point basics

**Hands-On Activities:**
- Dataset: Farm input costs and sales revenues
- Exercise: Calculate profit for each crop
- Exercise: Compare profitability across crops
- Exercise: Identify which farms make the most profit

**Deliverable:**
- **Project 1B:** "Farm Profitability Analysis"
  - Excel farm budget template (simple)
  - Power BI dashboard with:
    - Revenue vs Cost by crop
    - Profit margin comparison
    - Most profitable crops
    - Cost breakdown
  - Recommendations (1 page)

**Tools:** Excel, Power BI

---

#### **Week 4: Agricultural Market Prices**

**üé§ LIVE SESSION #1: "Understanding Agricultural Markets"**
- **Guest:** Market Analyst or Agribusiness Professional
- **Format:** 60-minute Q&A (after interns analyze price data)
- **Focus:** How markets work, price patterns, when to sell

**What Interns Will Learn:**
- How agricultural prices work
- Farm gate vs wholesale vs retail prices
- Seasonal price changes (low during harvest, high during lean season)
- Price trends and patterns

**Self-Paced Research Topics:**
- Why do agricultural prices change?
- When is the best time for farmers to sell?
- How do middlemen affect prices?

**Power BI Focus:**
- Line charts for price trends
- Comparing prices across markets
- Seasonal pattern visualization
- Simple price forecasting

**Hands-On Activities:**
- Dataset: 1 year of weekly market prices for 5 crops
- Exercise: Calculate average prices by season
- Exercise: Identify price peaks and lows
- Exercise: Compare prices across different markets

**Deliverable:**
- **Project 1C:** "Market Price Analysis Dashboard"
  - Power BI dashboard with:
    - Price trends over time
    - Seasonal price patterns
    - Price comparison across markets
    - Farm gate vs market price gap
  - Market brief (2 pages): When to sell each crop

**Tools:** Power BI, SQL, Excel

**Week 4 Checkpoint:** Portfolio review with mentors

---

### **MONTH 2: SUPPLY CHAIN & FOOD SECURITY**

---

#### **Week 5: Post-Harvest & Storage**

**What Interns Will Learn:**
- What happens after harvest
- Post-harvest losses (spoilage, waste)
- Storage and transportation
- Supply chain basics

**Self-Paced Research Topics:**
- Why do crops spoil after harvest?
- How do farmers store their crops?
- What is post-harvest loss?

**SQL Focus:**
- Calculating loss percentages
- Tracking storage duration
- Analyzing transportation data

**Hands-On Activities:**
- Dataset: Harvest and storage records for 1000 farmers
- Exercise: Calculate post-harvest loss percentage
- Exercise: Identify where losses occur most
- Exercise: Analyze storage duration vs quality

**Deliverable:**
- **Project 2A:** "Post-Harvest Loss Analysis"
  - Power BI dashboard showing:
    - Loss rates by crop
    - Where losses happen (storage, transport, market)
    - Storage duration analysis
    - Regional loss comparison
  - Recommendations to reduce losses

**Tools:** Power BI, SQL, Excel

---

#### **Week 6: Food Security Basics**

**üé§ LIVE SESSION #2: "Food Security & Nutrition"**
- **Guest:** Food Security Analyst or NGO Program Officer
- **Format:** 60-minute session (after intern research)
- **Focus:** How to measure food security, real-world programs

**What Interns Will Learn:**
- What is food security?
- How to measure if people have enough food
- Food availability vs food access
- Basic nutrition indicators

**Self-Paced Research Topics:**
- What makes people food secure or insecure?
- How do we measure food availability?
- What is dietary diversity?

**Excel Focus:**
- Simple food security calculations
- Household food consumption analysis
- Food expenditure tracking

**Hands-On Activities:**
- Dataset: Household food security survey (1000 households)
- Exercise: Calculate food availability per household
- Exercise: Analyze food spending patterns
- Exercise: Identify food-insecure households

**Deliverable:**
- **Project 2B:** "Food Security Monitoring Dashboard"
  - Power BI dashboard with:
    - Food availability by region
    - Household food access levels
    - Food expenditure analysis
    - Geographic food security map
  - 2-page brief on priority areas

**Tools:** Power BI, SQL, Excel

---

#### **Week 7: Weather & Seasonal Patterns**

**What Interns Will Learn:**
- How weather affects farming
- Rainfall patterns and crop performance
- Understanding seasons
- Simple climate analysis

**Self-Paced Research Topics:**
- How does rain affect crops?
- What is a growing season?
- How do farmers adapt to weather changes?

**Power BI Focus:**
- Rainfall charts and trends
- Comparing weather and yield data
- Seasonal pattern visualization

**Hands-On Activities:**
- Dataset: 5 years of rainfall and crop yield data
- Exercise: Plot rainfall patterns
- Exercise: Compare high-rain vs low-rain years
- Exercise: Identify relationship between rain and yield

**Deliverable:**
- **Project 2C:** "Weather Impact Analysis"
  - Power BI dashboard with:
    - Annual rainfall patterns
    - Rainfall vs yield comparison
    - Good years vs bad years
    - Regional weather differences
  - Simple recommendations for farmers

**Tools:** Power BI, Excel

---

#### **Week 8: Farm Planning & Budgeting**

**üé§ LIVE SESSION #3: "Practical Farm Management"**
- **Guest:** Agricultural Extension Officer or Farm Manager
- **Format:** 45-minute practical session
- **Focus:** How farmers plan their seasons, budgeting tips

**What Interns Will Learn:**
- How farmers plan for the season
- Creating farm budgets
- Input planning (seeds, fertilizer)
- Expected income calculations

**Self-Paced Research Topics:**
- How do farmers plan their planting?
- What goes into a farm budget?
- How to help farmers improve profitability?

**Excel Focus:**
- Farm planning template
- Seasonal budget creation
- Input-output calculations
- Simple scenario planning

**Hands-On Activities:**
- Exercise: Create farm plan for next season
- Exercise: Budget for 2 different crops
- Exercise: Calculate expected profit
- Exercise: Compare different farming options

**Deliverable:**
- **Major Project 2:** "Farm Planning Tool"
  - Excel farm planning template
  - Seasonal budget calculator
  - Power BI dashboard:
    - Crop calendar
    - Budget summary
    - Expected profit scenarios
    - Input requirements
  - User guide for farmers (2 pages, simple language)

**Tools:** Excel, Power BI

**Week 8 Checkpoint:** Mid-program review
